==========================================================================
SUMMARY
==========================================================================

Building on the static profile of Paper I, this paper introduces the cascade-throttled mobility mu(R) and shows that the resulting steady-state clock-rate factor matches the leading expansion of the Schwarzschild factor sqrt(1 - 2GM/(r c^2)) to within numerical precision. Notably, the post-Newtonian correction emerges from the self-coupling of the field's own back-reaction --- the DDD analogue of gravity gravitating itself.

==========================================================================
WHY THIS MATTERS
==========================================================================

The Schwarzschild factor is the simplest visible signature of general relativity beyond Newton. Reproducing it from a discrete local rule on a fixed graph is a non-trivial consistency test for any emergent-gravity programme. The self-coupling that produces the correction is a structural feature of the drainage rule, not a fitted parameter.

==========================================================================
NOVELTY
==========================================================================

Cascade-throttled mobility mu(R). Numerical demonstration of the leading post-Newtonian correction. Analytical derivation of the clock-rate factor in the weak-field limit. Comparison with Regge-calculus and CDT approaches.
